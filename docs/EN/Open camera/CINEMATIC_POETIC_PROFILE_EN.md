# 🎬 CINEMATIC POETIC PROFILE — OPPO Reno 11F

## 🌫️ 1. GENERAL IDEA (MORE IMPORTANT THAN SETTINGS)

You're not capturing an "event," but:
- light conditions
- pauses between actions
- the breath of space
- random moments of life
👉 rule:
> "Don't capture everything — leave some breathing room in the frame"

## 📷 2. CAMERA (Open Camera / Manual Style)

### 🎞 SHOOTING MODE
* 4K 24fps (if not available, 30fps)
* 24fps gives a cinematic "breathing"

### 🌗 EXPOSURE (the key to poetry)
**✔ Exposure compensation (EV)**
* daylight: -0.3 … -0.7
* soft Light/Fog: -0.3
* sunset: -0.7 (very important)
👉 meaning:
you don't let the image be too honest, you darken it like a movie.

### 🌫 ISO (anti-digital noise)
* daytime: 100–200
* clouds: 200–400
* evening: 400–800 (no higher)
👉 above 800 → the poetics breaks (a "dirty digital" effect appears)

### 🎥 SHUTTER (movement like a movie)
* 24fps → 1/48 or 1/50
* 30fps → 1/60
👉 cinema rule:
> shutter speed ≈ 2x fps

## 🎨 3. COLOR (HEART) POETRY)

### 🌤 WHITE BALANCE (very important)
Not Auto.

Fix:
```text
| Scene | Kelvin |
| ------------ | ---------- |
| Daylight | 5200K |
| Warm Evening | 6000–6500K |
| Lamps | 3200–3800K |
```

👉 fixed WB = no "color breathing" (it kills the film)

### 🌈 EXPOSURE AESTHETICS
You imitate "film underexposure":
* the scene is slightly darker
* the highlights aren't blown out
* shadows retain their structure
👉 OPPO does this automatically, you do it manually

## 🧠 4. REMOVING DIGITAL "COLOR BLUR"

### ❌ disable / minimize:
* Noise reduction → LOW or OFF
* Edge enhancement → LOW
* Auto HDR → carefully (moderate is better)
👉 why:
strong denoise = "color spots" + skin texture is plasticky

## 🌫️ 5. CINEMATIC DEPTH

### 🎯 Focus
* Continuous AF (if the scene is moving)
* Lock focus (if the scene is poetic/static)
👉 poetics = less “focus hunting”

### 🪶 CAMERA BEHAVIOR
Don't keep everything in sharp focus:
* allow a slight softness
* allow understatement

## 🌌 6. LIGHT (THE MOST IMPORTANT THING IN POETICS)

You don't photograph the subject, but the light:

### best conditions:
* sunrise (soft cool)
* sunset (long shadows)
* cloudy day (even scene)
* evening with streetlights (contrast of life)

## 🎞 7. “CINEMATIC FRAME RECIPE”

Every frame:
1. first the light
2. then the action
3. then the person/object
👉 not the other way around

## 🧩 8. HOW IT WORKS WHAT IT LOOKS LIKE

You get:
- soft gradients without "spots"
- slightly underexposed frames (cinematic drama)
- natural leather without plastic
- a sense of "living air"
- visually closer to documentary art film

## 🎬 9. PLUS FOR YOUR EDITING (Shotcut)

In editing (Shotcut):
* Contrast: +5…+12
* Saturation: -5 or neutral
* Shadows: slightly down
* Highlights: slightly down
* Fade-in/out (very important)
👉 and most importantly:
don't "overpaint" the material—it should already be beautiful

## 💡 BRIEF PHILOSOPHY OF THIS PRESET

> "Beauty is not in the brightness, but in what remains in the shadows"
